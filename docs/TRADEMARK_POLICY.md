# Colomin Trademark and Branding Policy (Draft)

This document explains how the Colomin name, logo, and brand assets may be used.

## 1. What Is Covered

The following are treated as Colomin trademarks/brand assets:
- Product name: Colomin
- Project logo and icon assets
- Official visual identity and branding elements

## 2. Allowed Use (No Permission Needed)

You may use the name Colomin in plain text for factual reference, such as:
- "Works with Colomin"
- "Fork of Colomin"
- Comparative discussions and tutorials

You may also link to the official repository and releases.

## 3. Use That Requires Permission or Commercial Agreement

You must have permission (or a commercial agreement) to:
- Use the Colomin logo/icon in distributed products
- Distribute builds presented as official Colomin releases
- Use Colomin marks in a way that suggests endorsement, partnership, or certification
- Use the Colomin name in company/product branding that causes confusion

## 4. Forks and Modified Builds

Forks are welcome under the open-source license terms, but forks must:
- Use a clearly different product name
- Remove or replace official Colomin logos/icons by default
- Avoid wording that implies the fork is the official Colomin release

Recommended wording:
- "Based on Colomin"
- "Unofficial fork of Colomin"

## 5. Domain, App Store, and Package Naming

Do not register domains, app listings, or package names that imply official Colomin ownership unless explicitly authorized.

## 6. Enforcement

The maintainer may request changes to naming/branding usage that is misleading.
Failure to comply may result in revocation of branding permission.

## 7. Contact

For trademark/branding permissions, contact the Colomin maintainer.

## 8. Status

This is a policy draft for repository guidance and is not legal advice.
Final legal language should be reviewed by legal counsel.
